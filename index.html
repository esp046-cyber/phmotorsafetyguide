üìãPre-Ride Checklist

  • Check tire pressure and tread depth
  • Test brakes (front and rear)
  • Inspect lights, signals, and horn
  • Check fluid levels (oil, brake, coolant)
  • Inspect chain tension and lubrication
  • Ensure mirrors are properly adjusted
  • Check for loose bolts or parts
  • Verify registration and insurance documents

üéØDefensive Riding Mindset

Sa Pilipinas, defensive riding saves lives. Always assume other drivers cannot see you.

  • Visibility: Ride with headlights on, wear bright/reflective gear
  • Space: Maintain 2-second following distance minimum
  • Position: Stay in the left tire track for better visibility
  • Anticipate: Watch for opening car doors, pedestrians, and road hazards
  • Escape Routes: Always identify your "out" in traffic

üîßBasic Motorcycle Maintenance

Daily Checks (T-CLOCS)

  • T - Tires: Check pressure, tread, cracks (32-36 PSI typical)
  • C - Controls: Throttle, clutch, brakes responsive
  • L - Lights: Headlight, taillight, signals, brake light
  • O - Oil: Check level, look for leaks
  • C - Chassis: Check for loose parts, leaks
  • S - Stands: Side stand, center stand secure

Weekly Maintenance

  • Clean and lubricate chain (every 500km)
  • Check chain tension (2-3cm play)
  • Wash bike (prevents rust, improves visibility)
  • Check brake pad thickness

Monthly Maintenance

  • Check battery terminals (clean if corroded)
  • Inspect brake fluid level
  • Check coolant level
  • Tighten all bolts and fasteners
  • Inspect cables for fraying

üí° Change oil every 1,000-2,000km or 3 months for Manila traffic conditions

üéìRiding Skills to Practice

Practice these in safe, empty areas regularly:

  • Emergency Braking: Practice stopping quickly without locking wheels
  • Slow Speed Maneuvers: U-turns, figure-8s, tight circles
  • Swerving: Quick lane changes to avoid obstacles
  • Counter-steering: Push right handlebar to go right (at speed)
  • Head Checks: Looking over shoulder without swerving
  • One-handed riding: For signaling (practice at slow speeds)

‚ö†Ô∏è Never practice on public roads with traffic. Find empty parking lots or closed areas.

ü™ñEssential Safety Gear

1. Helmet (Mandatory in PH)

Requirements: DOT, ECE, or Snell certified full-face helmet

  • Replace every 5 years or after any impact
  • Must fit snugly without pressure points
  • Chin strap should allow only 2 fingers underneath

‚ö†Ô∏è "Tropang helmet" (P200-500 uncertified helmets) offer NO protection. Invest in your life!

2. Riding Jacket

  • Abrasion-resistant material (leather or textile)
  • CE-certified armor at shoulders, elbows, back
  • Bright colors or reflective panels for visibility
  • Mesh jackets for PH heat with armor

3. Gloves

Protect hands in 90% of crashes. Look for:

  • Palm sliders and knuckle protection
  • Reinforced stitching
  • Secure wrist closure

4. Riding Pants/Jeans

  • Kevlar-reinforced riding jeans minimum
  • Knee and hip armor pockets
  • Never wear shorts or regular jeans

5. Boots

  • Over-the-ankle height minimum
  • Oil-resistant, non-slip soles
  • Toe and heel protection
  • Avoid tsinelas or sneakers

üõ°Ô∏èGear Maintenance Tips

Helmet Care

  • Clean visor with mild soap, microfiber cloth (no harsh chemicals)
  • Wash inner padding every 2-3 months
  • Store in cool, dry place (not in direct sunlight)
  • Never hang on mirror (damages inner foam)
  • Replace after 5 years or any impact

Jacket & Pants Care

  • Remove armor before washing
  • Hand wash or gentle cycle in cold water
  • Air dry only (no dryer)
  • Re-waterproof annually with spray treatment
  • Check armor fitment regularly

Gloves Care

  • Hand wash with mild soap
  • Stuff with newspaper while drying to maintain shape
  • Condition leather monthly with leather conditioner
  • Replace when padding compresses or stitching fails

Boots Care

  • Clean after every ride (especially in rain)
  • Use leather conditioner on leather boots
  • Check sole grip regularly, replace if worn
  • Waterproof with appropriate spray treatment

üõ£Ô∏èCity Riding (Manila/Metro Areas)

  • Lane Splitting: Legal but use extreme caution. Stay under 40kph when filtering
  • Watch for buses/jeeps: They stop suddenly without warning
  • Avoid blind spots: Never ride beside large vehicles for long
  • Traffic lights: Be first or last in lane, never between cars
  • EDSA/C5: Use leftmost lane when safe, middle lanes have most hazards

üèîÔ∏èProvincial/Highway Riding

  • Watch for crossing animals, especially at dawn/dusk
  • Slow down for small towns (speed bumps often unmarked)
  • Be visible: many provincial roads lack lighting
  • Plan fuel stops (some areas have 50km+ between stations)
  • Check weather and road conditions before mountain rides

üåôNight Riding Safety

‚ö†Ô∏è Accident rates triple at night in PH

  • Reduce speed by 20-30% compared to daytime
  • Use high beam when no oncoming traffic
  • Wear reflective vest or gear
  • Avoid overtaking unless absolutely necessary
  • Watch for unlit vehicles/obstacles
  • Never ride if drowsy - pull over and rest

üö¶Intersection Safety

70% of motorcycle accidents in PH happen at intersections

  • Cover your brakes: Fingers on levers when approaching
  • Make eye contact: Ensure drivers see you
  • Watch for left-turning vehicles: They often don't see motorcycles
  • Position yourself: Be visible in driver's mirrors
  • Never assume right-of-way: Better late than injured

üõëBraking Techniques

  • 70/30 Rule: Front brake 70%, rear 30% for maximum stopping power
  • Progressive braking: Squeeze gradually, don't grab
  • Both brakes: Always use both together
  • Look where you want to go: Not at the obstacle
  • Practice emergency stops: In safe area weekly

üöõDealing with Large Vehicles

Trucks, buses, and trailers are major hazards:

  • Blind spots: If you can't see their mirrors, they can't see you
  • Never ride alongside: They can't see you and may merge
  • Pass quickly: Don't linger beside large vehicles
  • Wide turns: Trucks swing wide, give them space
  • Stopping distance: Stay far back, they take longer to stop
  • Debris: Loose cargo, tire fragments - maintain distance

‚ö†Ô∏è Never pass on the right side of trucks making right turns!

‚õΩFuel Efficiency Tips

Save money and extend range:

  • Maintain proper tire pressure (improves MPG by 10%)
  • Smooth throttle control (no aggressive acceleration)
  • Anticipate stops (coast to red lights instead of braking late)
  • Remove unnecessary weight from bike
  • Keep chain properly lubricated
  • Regular engine tune-ups
  • Ride at consistent speeds (60-80kph is most efficient)

üí° Typical motorcycle: 30-50 km/L city, 40-60 km/L highway

üë•Group Riding Safety

Formation Types

  • Staggered (most common): Left-right-left pattern, 2 second gap
  • Single file: For twisty roads, narrow lanes, bad weather
  • Side-by-side: NEVER do this in PH roads

Group Riding Rules

  • Pre-ride briefing: Route, stops, hand signals, pace
  • Ride your own ride: Don't exceed your skill level
  • Lead rider sets pace (should be slowest/least experienced)
  • Sweep rider at back (most experienced, helps stragglers)
  • Pass hazards one at a time, not as group
  • Everyone responsible for rider behind them
  • Use hand signals (covered below)

Common Hand Signals

  • Left turn: Left arm extended straight
  • Right turn: Left arm up at 90¬∞ or right arm extended
  • Stop: Left arm down at 45¬∞, palm back
  • Slow down: Left arm extended down, palm down, pump up/down
  • Hazard (left): Left foot point out
  • Hazard (right): Right foot point out
  • Single file: Left arm up, index finger extended
  • Follow me: Left arm bent up, swing forward
  • You lead: Left arm bent up, swing forward then point ahead
  • Fuel stop: Point to tank
  • Comfort stop: Arm bent, shake fist

‚ö†Ô∏è Never feel pressured to keep up with faster riders. Ride at YOUR safe pace!

üåßÔ∏èRiding in Rain

‚ö†Ô∏è First 30 minutes of rain is most dangerous (oil rises to surface)

  • Reduce speed by 50%: Stopping distance doubles on wet roads
  • Smooth inputs: Gentle throttle, braking, and steering
  • Avoid painted lines: Extremely slippery when wet
  • Increase following distance: 4-5 seconds minimum
  • Use both brakes gently: More rear brake than normal
  • Stay relaxed: Tense muscles lead to jerky movements

Rain Gear Essentials

  • Waterproof jacket and pants
  • Waterproof gloves or glove covers
  • Anti-fog visor treatment or pinlock
  • Waterproof boots or boot covers

‚òÄÔ∏èHot Weather Riding

PH summer temperatures reach 35-40¬∞C. Prevent heat exhaustion:

  • Wear mesh/ventilated jacket with armor (never ride without protection)
  • Stay hydrated: drink water every 30-45 minutes
  • Wear moisture-wicking base layers
  • Avoid riding during peak heat (12-3 PM) if possible
  • Take breaks in shade every hour
  • Watch for signs: dizziness, nausea, confusion = pull over immediately

üí®Strong Wind/Bagyo Season

‚ö†Ô∏è Avoid riding in Signal 2 or higher. Not worth the risk!

  • Strong crosswinds can push you into another lane
  • Grip tank with knees for stability
  • Reduce speed significantly
  • Watch for flying debris
  • Be extra cautious near large vehicles (wind buffeting)
  • If wind is too strong, pull over and wait it out

üåäFlooded Roads (Baha)

Rule #1: If you can't see the road surface, don't cross!

  • Maximum safe depth: ankle-deep (15-20cm)
  • Watch other vehicles first to gauge depth
  • Ride slowly and steadily (don't stop mid-crossing)
  • Keep revs up to prevent water entering exhaust
  • Dry brakes after crossing: pump them gently several times
  • Check air filter and oil after riding through flood water

üí° If engine dies in water: DO NOT restart! Push to dry area and have it checked.

üå´Ô∏èFog & Low Visibility Riding

Common in Tagaytay, Baguio, and mountain areas:

  • Use low beam (high beam reflects back, reduces visibility)
  • Reduce speed by 50% or more
  • Use road edge as guide, not center line
  • Increase following distance to 5+ seconds
  • Turn on hazard lights if moving very slowly
  • If visibility drops to zero, pull over completely off road
  • Wear reflective gear/vest

‚ö†Ô∏è Never stop in the road. If you must stop, get completely off the roadway!

üå°Ô∏èExtreme Heat Protection

Signs of Heat Exhaustion

  • Heavy sweating or no sweating
  • Dizziness, nausea, headache
  • Muscle cramps
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Confusion, irritability

Prevention

  • Drink 250ml water every 30 minutes
  • Avoid caffeine and alcohol before riding
  • Wear light-colored, ventilated gear
  • Take 10-minute breaks every hour
  • Wet bandana under helmet
  • Plan rides for early morning or late afternoon

If You Feel Heat Exhaustion

  1. Stop riding immediately
  2. Move to shade
  3. Remove helmet and jacket
  4. Drink water slowly
  5. Pour water on head, neck, wrists
  6. If symptoms persist, seek medical help

üëÆDealing with Traffic Enforcers

  • Be polite and respectful at all times
  • Know your rights: ask for violation specifics
  • Request citation ticket (never pay cash on the spot)
  • Take note of enforcer's name and ID number
  • If wrongfully flagged, file complaint with agency
  • Never argue aggressively - it won't help

üé´Paying Traffic Violations

How to Pay Properly

  1. Receive Traffic Violation Receipt (TVR) from enforcer
  2. Pay within 7 days at authorized payment centers
  3. Options: LTO offices, Bayad Centers, online via LTO portal
  4. Bring valid ID and TVR
  5. Get official receipt as proof

‚ö†Ô∏è Paying "under the table" is illegal and encourages corruption. Always ask for official receipt!

What to Do if Apprehended

  • Stay calm and cooperative
  • Ask officer to explain the violation
  • Present your documents: license, OR/CR
  • If you agree with violation, accept ticket
  • If you disagree, still accept ticket but note your objection
  • You can contest the ticket later at adjudication

üÜîGetting Your Motorcycle License

Requirements (Restriction Code 1)

  • 18 years old or above
  • Valid Student Permit (held for at least 1 month)
  • Pass Practical Driving Exam (PDE)
  • Medical certificate
  • Drug test result

Practical Driving Test Includes

  • Pre-departure inspection
  • Starting and stopping
  • Figure-8 pattern
  • Slalom course
  • Emergency braking
  • Road rules knowledge

License Validity

  • New license: 5 years
  • Renewal: 5 years (if no violations)
  • Can renew up to 1 year before expiry
  • 10-year license available for clean records

üí° Take a rider safety course even if not required. It can save your life!

üîíAnti-Carnapping Tips

Motorcycle theft is common in PH. Protect your investment:

  • Always use multiple locks: Disc lock + chain + cover
  • Park in well-lit, visible areas
  • Use GPS tracker: Hidden location, not obvious spots
  • Never leave key in ignition: Even for "just a minute"
  • Lock steering: Turn handlebar all the way, lock
  • Remove valuables: Don't leave phone, wallet in compartments
  • Install alarm system: Loud deterrent for thieves
  • Mark bike with unique identifiers: UV pen on hidden parts
  • Take photos: Document serial numbers, unique marks
  • Avoid parking in same spot daily: Thieves watch patterns

If Your Bike is Stolen

  1. Report to police immediately (get police report)
  2. Report to PNP-HPG Anti-Carnapping Unit
  3. Notify LTO within 24 hours
  4. File insurance claim (if covered)
  5. Post on social media with photos, OR/CR details
  6. Check online marketplaces daily
  7. Alert local motorcycle groups

ü©πAccident Response Protocol

If You're Involved in an Accident:

  1. Safety First: Move to safe area if possible, turn on hazards
  2. Check for Injuries: Your safety and others'
  3. Call for Help: 911 or emergency contacts
  4. Don't Move Seriously Injured: Wait for medical professionals
  5. Document Everything: Photos of scene, damages, injuries, plates
  6. Get Witness Info: Names, contact numbers, statements
  7. Exchange Information: Driver details, insurance, OR/CR
  8. File Police Report: Even for minor accidents
  9. Notify Insurance: Within 24 hours
  10. Don't Admit Fault: Let authorities determine liability

üîßRoadside Breakdown

  • Pull over to safest spot possible
  • Turn on hazard lights immediately
  • Set up early warning device (EWD) 5 meters behind bike
  • Move away from traffic while waiting for help
  • Call roadside assistance or trusted mechanic
  • If nighttime, use flashlight/reflector for visibility

Common Quick Fixes

  • Dead battery: Push-start (if possible) or call for jump
  • Flat tire: Use emergency plug kit, inflate, ride slowly to shop
  • Chain off: Re-attach (if not damaged), check tension
  • Overheating: Stop immediately, let cool 15-20 min, check coolant

üè•Basic First Aid

‚ö†Ô∏è This is basic guidance only. Always seek professional medical help for serious injuries.

For Road Rash/Abrasions:

  • Clean wound with clean water immediately
  • Apply antiseptic (betadine, alcohol)
  • Cover with sterile bandage
  • Change dressing daily
  • Watch for signs of infection (redness, pus, fever)

For Bleeding:

  • Apply direct pressure with clean cloth
  • Elevate injured area above heart
  • Don't remove cloth if blood soaks through, add more on top
  • Seek medical help if bleeding doesn't stop in 10 minutes

For Fractures/Broken Bones:

  • DO NOT move the injured person unless in danger
  • Immobilize the injured area
  • Call 911 immediately
  • Cover open wounds with clean cloth
  • Watch for shock (pale, cold, rapid breathing)
